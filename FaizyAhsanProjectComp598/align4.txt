#=GF CC   The hammerhead ribozyme is one of the smallest catalytic RNAs. These RNAs
#=GF CC   have an endonuclease function, and most often are autocatalytic.
#=GF CC   Structurally it is composed of three base paired helices, separated by
#=GF CC   short linkers of conserved sequence. These helices are called I, II and
#=GF CC   III. We have classified hammerhead ribozymes into three types based on
#=GF CC   which helix the 5' and 3' ends of the sequence join. This family are the
#=GF CC   type I hammerheads. The conserved uridine-turn links helix I to helix II
#=GF CC   and usually has the sequence CUGA. Helix II and III are linked by a
#=GF CC   sequence CGAAA. The cleavage reaction occurs between helix III and I, and
#=GF CC   is usually a C. Hammerhead ribozymes are found in plant viroids and other
#=GF CC   small replicating satellite RNA species. Hammerhead ribozymes have been
#=GF CC   found in animals as well as plants.
